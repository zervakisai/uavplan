# Ethics & Responsible Use — UAVBench

## Scope and Intent

UAVBench is a **benchmarking framework** for evaluating path-planning algorithms
in simulated 2D urban grid environments. It is designed exclusively for
**academic research** in robotics, AI, and operations research. The benchmark
does not control real UAVs, does not interface with flight-control hardware,
and does not produce deployable navigation software.

## Dual-Use Awareness

UAVBench evaluates planners under both **civil** (wildfire response, maritime
patrol, critical infrastructure inspection) and **defense-adjacent** (border
surveillance, comms-denied operations) mission profiles. This dual-use framing
is intentional: it reflects the real-world spectrum of UAV applications and
enables researchers to study how planning algorithms behave across mission
semantics without building separate benchmarks for each domain.

### Safeguards

1. **Simulation only.** All scenarios run on synthetic 2D occupancy grids
   derived from OpenStreetMap. No real sensor data, no real flight plans,
   and no real-time control loops are involved.

2. **No weaponization pathway.** The benchmark evaluates path optimality,
   replanning responsiveness, and safety-constraint compliance — metrics
   that are agnostic to payload. The framework deliberately omits any
   targeting, tracking, or engagement logic.

3. **Transparency by design.** All scenario configurations, planner
   implementations, and evaluation protocols are open-source under the
   MIT license, enabling full scrutiny by the research community.

4. **Operational constraints as first-class citizens.** Every scenario
   enforces no-fly zones, safety buffers, and emergency corridors. The
   benchmark penalizes — rather than rewards — constraint violations.

## Privacy

UAVBench does not use, collect, or process any personal data. The population
risk maps are synthetic density fields, not derived from real census or
tracking data. No human subjects are involved in any part of the benchmark.

## Environmental Impact

The benchmark is CPU-only (no GPU required) and runs in under 60 minutes
for a full 30-seed validation on a laptop-class machine. We estimate the
carbon footprint of a single full run at < 0.01 kg CO₂e.

## Responsible Disclosure

If you identify a potential misuse vector or ethical concern related to
UAVBench, please contact the authors directly via the repository's issue
tracker or the email addresses listed in the paper.

## Recommended Citation Practice

When using UAVBench in published work, we ask that authors:

1. Cite the original UAVBench paper (see `CITATION.bib`).
2. Acknowledge the OpenStreetMap data source per ODbL requirements.
3. Clearly state that results are from simulated environments, not real flights.
